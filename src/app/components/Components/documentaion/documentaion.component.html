Core Functionalities

Student Role: Project Creation & Management

As a student, you are at the heart of the platform. Your primary role is to create and manage your graduation project from concept to completion.

Key Actions:

  • Create a New Project: From your dashboard, you can initiate a new project by providing a title, a detailed abstract, and selecting your department. This creates a dedicated workspace for your project.
  • Build Your Team: As the project leader, you can invite other students to join your team by searching for their academic ID. Team members will receive an invitation to accept.
  • Upload and Version Files: Submit all project-related files, including proposals, reports, source code, and presentations. The system maintains a version history for each submission.
  • View Feedback: Access comments and grades from your supervisor directly on the project page.
Supervisor Role: Guidance & Evaluation

As a supervisor (Doctor), you are responsible for guiding students and evaluating their work to ensure it meets academic standards.

Key Actions:

  • Dashboard Overview: Your dashboard displays all projects assigned to you for supervision, along with their current status and pending submissions.
  • Review Submissions: Access and download student files for each milestone. You can view the complete history of submissions.
  • Provide In-depth Feedback: Use the integrated commenting system to provide specific, actionable feedback on documents and code. This creates a formal record of all interactions.
  • Grade Milestones: Assign grades and approve or request revisions for each project milestone, moving the project through its lifecycle.
Note: Supervisors can only view and interact with projects explicitly assigned to them by the administration.
Administrator Role: Platform Control

The Administrator has the highest level of access and is responsible for the overall management and configuration of the platform.

Key Actions:

  • User Management: Create, modify, and deactivate accounts for students, supervisors, and other administrators.
  • Full Project Access: View, edit, and manage any project on the system. This includes changing project details, reassigning supervisors, or modifying team members if necessary.
  • System Configuration: Set academic year dates, define submission deadlines, configure grading schemas, and manage department categories.
  • Reporting & Analytics: Generate comprehensive reports on platform usage, project completion rates, and user activity.
